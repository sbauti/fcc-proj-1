Glimps of a life journey of Mahatma Gandhi

  • Birth in Porbandar (1869)- Mohandas Karamchand Gandhi was born on October 2, 1869, in Porbandar, Gujarat, into a devout Hindu family. His upbringing instilled values of honesty and compassion.
  • Marriage to Kasturba Gandhi (1883)- At the age of 13, Gandhi married Kasturba. Their relationship became a cornerstone of his personal and public life, emphasizing equality and mutual support.
  • Studies in England (1888–1891)- Gandhi traveled to London to study law at University College. This period marked his exposure to Western ideas, vegetarianism, and the Bhagavad Gita's teachings.
  • Transformation in South Africa (1893–1915)- As a young lawyer in South Africa, Gandhi faced racial discrimination, which led him to develop and practice nonviolent resistance, or Satyagraha, for social justice.
  • Return to India (1915)- Gandhi returned to India, becoming a central figure in the struggle for independence. He advocated for Swadeshi goods, self-reliance, and rural development.
  • Champaran Satyagraha (1917)- Gandhi led his first significant Indian protest in Champaran, Bihar, fighting for indigo farmers' rights against exploitative British landlords, marking a turning point in India's freedom movement.
  • Salt March (1930)- Gandhi led the Dandi March, a 240-mile walk to protest British salt taxes. It became a powerful symbol of nonviolent resistance and self-sufficiency.
  • Quit India Movement (1942)- Gandhi called for the British to leave India entirely. His leadership inspired mass civil disobedience, despite his imprisonment during this critical phase of India's struggle.
  • India’s Independence (1947)- After decades of struggle, India gained independence. However, Gandhi was heartbroken by the partition and violence that followed, advocating unity and peace until his death.
  • Assassination (1948)- On January 30, 1948, Gandhi was assassinated by Nathuram Godse. His death marked the end of an era, but his ideals of peace and truth continue to inspire the world.
